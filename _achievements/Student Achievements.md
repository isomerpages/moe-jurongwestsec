<p><strong>National Poetry Competition 2022 Prize Winners</strong>  </p>
<p>The National Poetry Competition is an annual poetry writing event organised by Poetry Festival Singapore and the National Arts Council. The competition invited aspiring poets of all ages across Singapore to write an original work on the theme of “Resurgence”. A total of 23 JWSS Literature students participated in this competition, displaying their deep passion in poetry, and applying what they have learnt from the subject to their creative works.&nbsp;</p>
<p>Among an estimated 370 entries received, two of our students, Nidhi Somashekar from 1 Integrity, and Valentina Anburaj from 1 Respect, managed to clinch the 3rd Prize and Merit Award in the Super Junior Category, respectively.</p>
<p><img src="/images/2022studentahievements.jpg"></p>
<p>Our prize winners, Nidhi Somashekar (1IY) and Valentina Anburaj (1RT), with their certificates at the awards ceremony on 30 July 2022  </p>
<p>-</p>
<p>‘Sun Mother’ by Nidhi Somashekar (1IY)  </p>
<p><em>Space, a vacuum, hollow and empty,</em></p>
<p><em>Gases dance and swirl, Hydrogen and helium adrift,</em></p>
<p><em>Swirl and dance in a symphony.</em></p>
<p><em>After all, what is there to witness?</em></p>
<p><em>For a spectacle like this has happened</em></p>
<p><em>A trillion times before.</em></p>
<p><em>Whispers and then boom!</em></p>
<p><em>Resurgence, Sun Mother is born.</em></p>
<p><em>Hydrogen and helium from many stars before,</em></p>
<p><em>have granted us life,</em></p>
<p><em>A renewed star, sun mother,</em></p>
<p><em>Has formed.</em></p>
<p><em>On planet Earth,</em></p>
<p><em>magma and rock,</em></p>
<p><em>Ashen air and red like blood.</em></p>
<p><em>Then, life.</em></p>
<p><em>But life always ends,</em></p>
<p><em>In months, decades or millenniums.</em></p>
<p><em>But the Sun is immortal,</em></p>
<p><em>Or so we think.</em></p>
<p><em>Sun mother gave us life,</em></p>
<p><em>The atmosphere and rich earth soil.</em></p>
<p><em>From microscopic bacteria,</em></p>
<p><em>Millions of years later,</em></p>
<p><em>Us, humans, came to be.</em></p>
<p><em>We harnessed its energy,</em></p>
<p><em>Learnt to weave its bright rays,</em></p>
<p><em>Into machines,</em></p>
<p><em>into spectacular inventions.</em></p>
<p><em>But the sun will not last forever,</em></p>
<p><em>Death awaits from afar,</em></p>
<p><em>Slowly and slowly,</em></p>
<p><em>It expands, inflates and becomes a giant ball of hot plasma.</em></p>
<p><em>And unforgivingly devours Mercury and Venus,</em></p>
<p><em>spares Earth with nothing but harsh rays.</em></p>
<p><em>Life disappears as fast as it was created.</em></p>
<p><em>In its void, lies her corpse, and a stunning, breathtaking nebula,</em></p>
<p><em>Sun mother has succumbed to time.</em></p>
<p><em>Will she resurge and become a new star, no one knows.</em></p>
<p><em>But now, her lifetime is over,</em></p>
<p><em>Or so we think.</em></p>
<p>*</p>
<p>The Sun has always been the gemstone of our Solar System and without its spectacular presence we would not have come to existence. This celestial body has been an intricate part of forming life and its existence intrigues people to this day. It was created by a corpse of a previous star and its revival spelled hope for life to thrive.</p>
<p>*</p>
<p>‘Winter Sleep’ by Valentina Anburaj (1RT)  </p>
<p>You were spring to me, and so I’ll be alright now, Love.</p>
<p>A voice reciting my name, warm and clear, with no question mark.</p>
<p>A canopy of white Lilac petals falling,</p>
<p>an Ivory climax of blushing innocence and youth.</p>
<p>Spring, nothing else you could have been, you must have been spring.</p>
<p>An aching, intoxicating heat brushing everyone’s cheeks</p>
<p>as pollen causes my eyes to itch.</p>
<p>Spring, certainly spring.</p>
<p>Made me pour out a cup of drowsy Winter, and mark a crisp sheet of Autumn,</p>
<p>eyes met you and it changed the world into something new.</p>
<p>By the providence of the star-glazed Universe, you called and I became your bloom.</p>
<p>Shall we meet at the next stage?</p>
<p>I need to face you again before I melt away too.</p>
<p>A promise carried in the sweet tune of a Pine-green orgel box,</p>
<p>the breath of serendipity and hope- a saving Penicillin.</p>
<p>Your existence demands poetry,</p>
<p>and I'm fussing through the night, trying to get the lines just right,</p>
<p>because these more pleasant nights only come by - to stir life - once in a while.</p>
<p>Blinding city signs and lights return and I'm obliged to work again.</p>
<p>No more depressing excuse to eat nothing but grapefruits and medication,</p>
<p>no excuse to lay supine all day long regardless of anyone's banging clinomania.</p>
<p>Had an urge like that of an undying flame to stay in this little bubble bowl,</p>
<p>but you came in and forced a flower arrangement to bloom around me,</p>
<p>Lavenders, Spireas, Buttercups, Dandelions and Clematis.</p>
<p>You ignited my flame of will to live with kerosene,</p>
<p>and fed it with the dull images that I lived through previously.</p>
<p>A decalcomania in me of the euphoria you cause,</p>
<p>the utopia you were,</p>
<p>fully brought back for all the children to hold hands with and find a meaning in.</p>
<p>A revival of life, a revival of joy, of untold truth, of purpose, of anticipation;</p>
<p>a revival of me.</p>
<p>It’s a new chapter with Déjà vu,</p>
<p>and I’m just praying through dry lips which host missing fibres that you’ll stay,</p>
<p>to show you me, give you me, and to accept and love me.</p>
<p>It’s a game of See-Saw, leaving one state then entering it again-</p>
<p>Could you tell me how it all leads back to you?</p>
<p>I’m meeting the prior page at the next stop,</p>
<p>still in a bed of early blossoms no longer stuck in concrete,</p>
<p>glad to not be stuck in my stigmatic Winter sleep again, while whispering,</p>
<p>“Hi, Spring- Bye.”</p>

<p>'Winter Sleep' details the revival of life after a harsh winter as the speaker overcomes a seasonal depression episode and returns to normal life. The people around him also become livelier and more euphoric as they rouse from the slumber of winter. It hopes to convey that all good days come back eventually.  </p>

<p>Their poems are published on Poetry Festival Singapore’s website, along with the other winning entries. You may visit the website here:&nbsp;<a href="https://www.poetryfestival.sg/national-poetry-competition-2022">https://www.poetryfestival.sg/national-poetry-competition-2022</a></p>
<p>Congratulations to our winners and good job to the students who have participated in this year’s competition!</p>

<h4 style="color:purple" align="center">KEEPSGCLEAN 2021 #CleanDesign</h4>	
&nbsp;<h4 style="color:purple" align="center">‘Feed My Trashemon’ School Competition</h4>
  

<p>In collaboration with the Ministry of Education (MOE), the Public Hygiene Council (PHC) conducted the inaugural nation-wide design competition “Feed My Trashemon” from 18 October to 30 November 2021. The competition aimed to raise awareness and encourage everyone to take action for a clean Singapore. Students with a passion for the arts were encouraged to showcase their creativity and imagination.</p>

  

<p>Our Art JeWel student, Gan Jing Wen, has come in first place out of the numerous high standard entries submitted by youths nation-wide in Singapore as assessed by PHC judging panel. Jing Wen’s work is published on PHC’s website. (<a href="https://www.publichygienecouncil.sg/resources/trashemon-school-competition">https://www.publichygienecouncil.sg/resources/trashemon-school-competition</a>).  </p>

<p>She would also be receiving $1,000 worth of prizes and a certificate at PHC’s flagship event, Keep Clean, Singapore! 2022 in April 2022. Jing Wen’s design would also be produced into an actual bin along with other winning entries. Congratulations, Jing Wen!</p>
